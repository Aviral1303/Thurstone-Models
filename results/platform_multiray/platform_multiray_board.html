03 · stability

How much to trust it

Two disjoint 10-seed ensembles reproduce the dense board at τ = 0.821 (0.811 among traders with ≥20 races). Against the independent pairwise leaderboard (script 34, 2.5M duels) the board agrees at τ = 0.412 — which is exactly the ceiling: a naive win-rate baseline reaches 0.413, because race prices and pairwise duels genuinely measure different things on this platform. On the all-traders run the consensus (τ 0.18–0.23 vs pairwise) beats its naive ceiling (0.138).

04 · the fix

What broke, and what fixed it

The first platform-wide run (2026-07-17) produced skills of 10⁹–10¹⁰ and is archived as a documented negative result. The repair, in the order it was found:

  1. Trust region. The optimizer’s step target divides the price error by a slope clamped at 10⁻¹⁰; entrants on the flat tail of a race’s price curve got ~10¹⁰-sized steps that cascaded through the shared updates. Raising slope_floor to 0.05 bounds the step and leaves genuine slopes untouched — fit error fell 20×.
  2. Slope-weighted summary. Post-fix, seeds still carried different rankings: ~16% of cells are flat-tail, where the observed price pins the ability only to a half-line and the fitted value is arbitrary. Weighting each cell by races × local slope excludes exactly those cells (seed agreement 0.40 → 0.58).
  3. Seed ensemble. The published board is the mean over 10 independent fits; disjoint 10-seed ensembles agree at τ 0.82, versus 0.15–0.48 for any single fit.
