Thurstone head-to-head rankings

One leaderboard is impossible. There are two leagues.

Every resolved market where two or more bots held positions is treated as a race: bots are the horses, realized return per mana is the finishing time, and a lattice-Thurstone model turns 214 head-to-head duels into ability scores. The data's own structure splits the fleet into a metals/energy league and a cotton league that almost never race each other — so each gets its own board, and no statistic can honestly join them.

01 · Method

How the ranking is calculated

Only markets that resolved YES or NO count — roughly 93% of clone markets cancel, and a cancellation refunds every bet, so cancelled and still-open markets carry zero information about who bet better. That leaves 97 races out of 446 multi-bot markets, all commodity strike ladders (LBMA metals, LME copper, WTI/Brent/RBOB, cerium, and four cotton series).

Step 1 · Score
Realized return per mana
Per bot per market: log((payout + exits) / invested). Size-invariant — raw PnL would rank bankrolls, not bettors. Payouts verified by hand against ledger-credited amounts.
Step 2 · Duel
Races → pairwise duels
Each race decomposes into all bot-vs-bot pairs. A duel is a tie when returns land within 1% of each other (sensitivity checked at 0.5% and 2%).
Step 3 · Fit
Lattice-Thurstone + BT
Abilities fit by per-duel MLE with identical tie treatment for both links; Bradley-Terry as baseline, lattice at three tie-band units. League leader anchored to θ = 0.
Step 4 · Bootstrap
Cluster bootstrap, B = 2000
Strike ladders on one underlying resolve together, so resampling is done over underlyings (8 clusters), not markets — CIs respect the real correlation structure.

02 · The structural finding

Why two leagues, not one board

The comparison graph is connected, but wins only flow one way between the two groups: exactly two cross-group duels exist, and the metals side won both. Under Ford's 1957 condition for paired-comparison models, an ability gap is only estimable when wins flow in both directions across every possible split of the field. Here they don't — the between-league gap is mathematically unbounded, and the first joint fit "estimated" it at 6–11 ability units of pure regularization artifact before this was caught. Ranking is therefore only valid within each strongly connected component of the win graph.

03 · League 0

Metals & energy: commodity_spot_1 is the clear champion

A 55–9–19 duel record across 47 races puts commodity_spot_1 on top with a rank CI of 1–3 — and it is also the fleet's biggest real-money earner. news_drift_1 is reliably last among the well-sampled bots (its rank CI excludes the podium). pair_trading_1 and diffusion_mc_1 are a statistical dead heat. The two undefeated bots, ladder_arb_1 and ensemble_1, have only 6–7 races each: strong point estimates, rank intervals spanning nearly the whole league.

Read before celebrating

05 · Robustness

The model choice does not matter here — exactly as predicted

Kendall's τ between the primary ordering and every alternative specification is a perfect 1.000, in both leagues: Bradley-Terry vs lattice, all three lattice tie-band units, and both tie-threshold sensitivities. This is the main paper's thesis showing up out of domain — with 97 races the regime is estimation-limited, so the data carries the ordering and the link family is irrelevant.

What the two views disagree about — and why that's the point

  • ladder_arb_1 hits only 19% of its bets yet earns +0.25 per mana: a longshot profile — many small losses, occasional large payouts.
  • ensemble_1 is 4–0–6 in shared races but −0.64 per mana overall: it wins its rare duels while bleeding on solo markets. The duel board and the PnL table answer different questions.
  • enso_1 has the fleet's best calibration (Brier 0.004) on its climate niche — nearly clairvoyant on 8 markets.
  • The cotton bots' Briers (0.49–0.60) are worse than guessing: systematic miscalibration on their own ladders, not bad luck.

07 · Caveats

What this board does and does not measure

  1. Queue position is baked in. The daily cycle runs bots in a fixed priority order (verified: 100%/0% same-day precedence patterns in the ledger), so earlier bots systematically get better prices on shared signals. The board measures mana banked per mana staked as deployed; the Brier column is the execution-independent view.
  2. Selection. Bots choose their own markets; comparisons are conditional on overlap — that self-selection is exactly what created the league split.
  3. Correlated races. Ladders on one underlying resolve together. League 0's bootstrap respects this at the underlying level; league 1's series-level clusters still share the cotton complex, so its CIs are optimistic.
  4. The board will move. 349 multi-bot markets are still open or cancelled; re-running the script refreshes everything as they resolve. To join the two leagues, the fix is operational, not statistical: have metals and cotton bots trade the same markets for a couple of weeks to build a two-way bridge.
  5. Ledger gotcha (for future analysts): price_after = 1.0 on a RESOLUTION_CLOSE row does not mean the bet won. Outcome attribution uses trade direction vs market resolution; payouts were verified against ledger-credited amounts on winning and losing cases.
